The best seasons to trek Mardi Himal are those with clear skies, moderate temperatures, and minimal rainfall. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

  1. Autumn (September to November) – Best Season

  • Weather: Clear skies, stable weather, and mild temperatures.
  • Views: Stunning mountain panoramas with crisp air—perfect for photography.
  • Trail conditions: Dry and safe; ideal for trekking.
  • Crowd: Popular season, so there may be more trekkers.

  1. Spring (March to May) – Another Great Option

  • Weather: Warm during the day, cooler at night.
  • Nature: Rhododendrons and other flowers bloom along the trail, adding vibrant colors.
  • Views: Good mountain visibility, though occasional haze may occur.
  • Trail conditions: Generally safe and dry; snow is mostly gone from lower elevations.

  1. Winter (December to February) – For Experienced Trekkers

  • Weather: Cold, especially at higher altitudes.
  • Views: Clear skies, sometimes snow-capped trails; excellent for mountain photography.
  • Trail conditions: Can be icy at higher camps; requires warm gear.
  • Crowd: Fewer trekkers, more solitude.

  1. Monsoon (June to August) – Not Recommended

  • Weather: Heavy rainfall and clouds.
  • Views: Mountains often hidden in clouds.
  • Trail conditions: Slippery and muddy trails; risk of landslides.
